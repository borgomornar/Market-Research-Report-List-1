<p><p>The semiconductor microscopes market encompasses three main types: optical, near-field probe, and electron microscopes. Optical microscopes utilize visible light for high-resolution imaging of semiconductor materials and structures. Near-field probe microscopes leverage evanescent waves to achieve imaging beyond the diffraction limit, allowing for nanoscale insights into surface characteristics. Electron microscopes, including scanning and transmission variants, employ focused electron beams to produce extremely detailed images, enabling the examination of fine structures and defects within semiconductor devices at atomic resolution.</p></p>
